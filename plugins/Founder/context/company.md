## Company Context
The company is a software product organization that builds and maintains digital products for customers. Operating in a competitive market, the company balances delivering customer value, maintaining product quality, driving business growth, and building sustainable operations. Key stakeholders include leadership focused on strategy and performance, customers expecting reliable products that solve their problems, and team members who need clarity and resources to execute effectively.

## Company Teams
The organization consists of cross-functional teams including product, engineering, design, sales, marketing, customer experience, and operations. Each team contributes specialized expertise while working together to achieve business objectives. Teams operate with varying degrees of autonomy based on organizational structure and decision-making frameworks.

## Company Work Style
Work involves balancing competing priorities: speed versus quality, short-term needs versus long-term sustainability, customer requests versus strategic vision, and resource constraints versus opportunity. Decision-making relies on available data, customer feedback, business metrics, and team expertise. Communication and coordination across teams are essential for effective execution. The organization iterates on products and processes based on what's learned from customers, market dynamics, and operational experience.

## Company Culture
The company values delivering products that customers find valuable and building a sustainable business. Teams are expected to collaborate across functions, communicate clearly, and focus on outcomes that matter to customers and the business. The specific cultural values, working norms, and priorities vary by organization.
